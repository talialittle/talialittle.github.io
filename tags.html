PHOTOS

How can a teacher provide the best feedback if they are unaware of what positions to look for? Below is each aerial broken down into photo segments. Practicing the correct form will allow for a safe completion of the skill and a happy dancer. The given terms below each image have been proven effective in increasing a dancer's understanding of the skill.

Side Aerials

.

One | The chest starts to drop at an angle

.

Two | The dropped arm swings through the inside of the leg

Three | Base leg pushes off the floor

Four | Back leg drive creates a big split and rotation

Five | Landing leg drives down as shoulders square

Six | Round back for easy stand-up

Front Aerials

.

One | Chest drops in a deep lunge

.

Two | Needle kick and lock eyes on floor

Three | Base leg pushes off the floor

Four | Back leg drive and arm circle creates rotation

Five | Back arch increases while chest starts to lift

Six | Arms follow last after chest and head rise

Back Aerials

.

One | Hips lean backward and arms rise

.

Two | Legs stay straight as hips rise

Three | Head looks for the floor as landing leg lifts

Four | Back rounds as landing leg drives toward the floor

Five | Landing leg makes contact

Six | Back rounds for easy stand-up
